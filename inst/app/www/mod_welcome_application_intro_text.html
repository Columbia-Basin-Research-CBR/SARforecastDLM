SARforecastDLM can be used to explore wild, spring/summer Snake River Chinook salmon, Oncorhynchus tshawytscha, one-year ahead forecasted survival based on changes in indicators of the ocean ecosystem within the Northern California Current (NCC).


This application utilizes dynamic linear modeling (DLM) to forecast salmon survival, incorporating time-varying parameters such as smolt-to-adult survival ratio (SAR), alongside long-term ocean ecosystem indicators including indices of upwelling and prey availability,

  • Coastal Upwelling Index (CUI), otherwise known as the Bakun Upwelling Index, (Bakun 1973,1975)
  • Coastal Upwelling Transport Index (CUTI), (Jacox et al 2018)
  • Index of Coastal Prey Biomass (ICPB), (Daly et al 2013)
Users can then select between three different estimates of SAR to explore changes in forecasted survival, such as:
  • 1964 to 2005 SAR reported in Scheurell and Williams (2005),
  • 2003 to present SAR reported by Columbia Basin Research, DART, and
  • 1994 to 2019 SAR estimates via Cormack-Jolly-Seber (CJS) modelling

To explore the application, select the Forecast Survival page in the side panel, or continue to the Overview section to learn more about how this application can assist is addressing knowledge-action gaps.

Please note that this Shiny App is dependent on data availability and serves as an exploratory tool.
